Why Visit?

Featuring: Top 3 Favorite Destinations

The Philippines is a tropical paradise with 7,641 stunning islands, offering pristine beaches, lush mountains, and vibrant marine life. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, this destination has it all.c

Explore world-famous beaches in Palawan and Boracay, surf in Siargao, or trek the Banaue Rice Terraces. Experience colorful festivals, historic sites, and warm Filipino hospitality.

Unique Experience

What truly sets the Philippines apart is the warmth and hospitality of its people, making every visitor feel at home. Beyond its breathtaking landscapes, the Philippines captivates with its welcoming people and rich cuisine. Enjoy iconic dishes like adobo and lechon while exploring bustling markets and scenic spots.

FAV SPOTS

3 Best places in the Philippines

Boracay Ocean Club

Famous for its powdery white sands and stunning sunsets, Boracay’s White Beach is a tropical paradise like no other. By day, relax on the shore or dive into thrilling water activities like parasailing and scuba diving. By night, experience its lively beachside nightlife with fire dancers, music, and world-class dining. Whether you seek relaxation or adventure, Boracay has it all.

Address

Station 3, Boracay Island, Malay, 5608 Aklan, Philippines

What I dont like about it

Over-tourism has led to overcrowding, especially during peak seasons. Despite recent rehabilitation efforts, environmental concerns and waste management remain challenges. Prices for accommodations, food, and activities tend to be higher than in other beach destinations in the Philippines. So, don't come on a peak season. Enjoy the place and also good for your wallet.

Location

Palawan Mandala Spa & Resort Villas

Often called the "Last Frontier," Palawan is a dream destination with its crystal-clear lagoons, dramatic limestone cliffs, and hidden beaches. Explore the enchanting underground river in Puerto Princesa, snorkel in the vibrant coral gardens of Honda Bay, or sail through the breathtaking islands of El Nido and Coron. Every moment in Palawan is a postcard-perfect adventure.

Address

Sabang, Puerto Princesa, Palawan Island 5300 Philippines

What I dont like about it

While Palawan is stunning, its popularity means some areas can get crowded, especially El Nido and Coron. Infrastructure in remote parts is still developing, leading to occasional power outages and limited internet connectivity. Additionally, getting around can be challenging due to long boat or van rides between attractions.

Location

SM Mall of Asia

SM Mall of Asia (MOA) is one of the largest shopping malls in the world, offering a vast selection of retail stores, dining options, and entertainment hubs. The mall’s seaside location provides stunning sunset views along Manila Bay, making it a great spot for leisurely walks. It also features an Olympic-sized skating rink, IMAX theater, and concert venues, ensuring there’s something for everyone. The mall is well-maintained, modern, and has a vibrant atmosphere, perfect for families and tourists alike.

Address

SM Mall of Asia, Seaside Blvd, Pasay, 1300 Metro Manila, Philippines

What I don't like about it

Despite its appeal, SM MOA can be overwhelmingly crowded, especially on weekends and holidays, making navigation difficult. The vast size means long walking distances between sections, which can be tiring. Traffic congestion around the area is also a major downside, with parking often a challenge. Additionally, while the mall offers high-end and budget-friendly options, some restaurants and shops are relatively pricey compared to other malls in the Philippines. So, just avoid going there on a weekend!
